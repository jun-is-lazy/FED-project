King of The Hill style competition at Singtel Comcentre

I remember this day, 20th May 2018 clearly since it was on my birthday.

It was also one day before my Chinese O level paper 1 and 2 LMAO (I still got A1 so it's fine)


If you were expecting a picture of my team and I: there isn't one! The organisers said that they would post it on the event's Facebook page but they didn't, so.

The facebook page in question: this and this

The prize: a black Razer Kraken V2 headset and a $10 Challenger voucher (which I gave to my mum lol).

Warning: huge text wall ahead. Click here for tl;dr.

King of The Hill style competition at Kallang Theatre

This one was in mid-December 2017..

I went to Kallang Theatre to watch the Vainglory 2017 World Championship.

Basically two Razer staff asked if I wanted to play in a competition too and told me that there would be prizes so I agreed.

It was a king-of-the-hill-style match which I didn't know about until the competition started, and since I was the first to sign up, I was also first in line to play.

When I looked behind me, there was a long queue snaking all the way to the entrance of the venue, and I thought, "Oh shit" but it was too late to back out, so I just carried on with it.

To my surprise, I easily carried my team and steamrolled over most of the other players. Yes, easily. At that time, I was spending hours everyday playing Vainglory, and I was ranked at Tier 9, Gold division, one rank away from the highest tier.

All was going smoothly, there were only 2 teams left between my team and the top prize. That was when a team of 3, all wearing matching jerseys, stood at the Razer phones across us. At a glance I knew they were different, and reality proved it to be true. They crushed me with their superior skill and team synergy just as I had done to the many teams before them.

Defeated, I could only accept the consolation prize, which was a Razer Hammerhead earpiece...for iOS. "I'm an Android user dammit", I thought, but there was nothing I could do.

Round 2:

The next day, I went again (to watch the competition of course), and I saw the same two Razer employees

Well they saw me too, so I waved to greet them as they approached me with somewhat apprehensive expressions on their faces.

I soon found out that the reason for their apprehension was because the team that crushed mine yesterday was made up of local pro players who were here with their team, Impunity.

Since they were only second-string pro players, they weren't sent to represent Singapore in the world championship, and instead came to participate in the unofficial one which I also happened to be in..

The thing is, they weren't actually allowed to play in the unofficial competition since they were pro players, and the Razer employees didn't notice that they were pro players, so they let them play. However, once they found out the truth about them being pro players, the employees immediately disqualified them.

However, by then, I was long gone, watching the championship within the huge crowd upstairs. Unable to find or contact me, they decided to hold the unofficial competition again the next day in hopes that my team would pass by again so that they could make it up to us.

As fate would have it, we did pass by just after the registration ended. The Razer employees decided to let us join at the end of the queue as they felt bad about the previous day.

When it was our turn, history repeated and we easily crushed the previous king of the hill. This time, we were fully rested, not exhausted from playing for nearly an hour without any breaks, and had ample time to prepare to destroy the reigning king while waiting in the queue.

TLDR:

I crushed noobs at a comp, then got crushed by pros; said pros were disqualified and I won $300+ worth of Razer goods in 2 days.

Yes this is a true story.
